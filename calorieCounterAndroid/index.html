ℹ️ How do these conditions affect your nutrition goals?
🦋 Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)

An underactive thyroid slows your metabolism, meaning your body burns fewer calories at rest. Eating the same amount as someone without the condition can lead to weight gain.

  • Calories: −200 kcal/day
  • Carbs: −30g/day
  • Fiber: +5g/day (helps digestion & satiety)
🦋 Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid)

An overactive thyroid speeds up your metabolism dramatically, causing rapid weight loss and muscle breakdown if not compensated with extra nutrition.

  • Calories: +300 kcal/day
  • Protein: +20g/day (prevents muscle loss)
  • Carbs: +30g/day (extra energy)
🩸 Diabetes

Diabetes impairs your body's ability to regulate blood sugar. High-carb meals cause dangerous blood sugar spikes. A lower-carb, higher-fiber diet helps maintain stable glucose levels.

  • Carbs: −50g/day (reduces blood sugar spikes)
  • Fiber: +5g/day (slows sugar absorption)
  • Fat: −10g/day (reduces cardiovascular risk)
❤️ High Blood Pressure

High blood pressure is worsened by excess sodium and saturated fat. Reducing fat intake and keeping sodium below 2300mg/day helps protect your heart and blood vessels.

  • Fat: −10g/day
  • Sodium: Limit to <2300mg/day (not tracked but recommended)
